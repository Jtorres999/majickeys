So now that you have booked a trip to Disney (or are still in the planning stages) let me tell you about the Disney World Parks (Orlando, Florida location). Disney World has 4 theme parks. For each of these parks you must have a park ticket (109.00 plus tax and price varies depending on the day) and if visiting one day you must pay for a parking ticket (varies from $30-$55), unless staying at the resorts (free parking). Each park is centered around different Disney movie themes, characters, and rides. I will be describing each park below. So, buckle up and enjoy the ride.

Magic Kingdom

	
	•Eat eggrolls and spring rolls at the Adventure Land Spring Roll Cart
	•Popcorn: They may be selling a collectible character popcorn bucket. They are adorable. 
	•Go to sleepy hollow and grab a filled waffle. 
	•Pick up Leflou’s Brew and a cinnamon roll at Gaston’s Tavern. 	
	•DOLE WHIP! If you like pineapple ice cream, pineapple upside down cake with pineapple ice cream and other varieties. Try dole whip. So delicious. You can find dole whip at the Aloha Isle or the Sunshine Tree Terrace.
	•Pick your Chesire tail up at Cheshire Cafe or the launching pad.

Epcot

	•You can stop by every country and try a little bit of food that they specialize in.
	•Visit Club Cool and try out new sodas from around the world. (Free samples)
	•Mickey Ice cream and Ice cream sandwiches. 
	•Known for drinking “around the world” where you can drink varieties of alcoholic beverages. Fun fact: the only park where you can walk around with an alcoholic beverage.

Animal Kingdom

	•Visit Pongu Pongu for a night blossom drink
	•You can grab dole whip here too. (Magic and Animal kingdom are the only parks you can find dole whip).

Hollywood Studios

	•Head to Blackspire Outpost to grab a cup of blue milk and green milk. 
	•Jack Jack Nun Bum cookie in Pixar Place. 
	•Grab a pop tart at Andy’s Toy Box

Magic Kingdom

	
	1.Happily Ever After - An amazing firework show that happens at 8:30 pm in front of Cinderella’s Castle. All ages are welcome. 
	2.Space Mountain - a roller coaster ride that is done in the dark and makes you feel like you are in space, For all individuals 44 inches and taller. 
	3.Buzz Light Year’s Space Ranger Spin- Slow spinning target shooting ride for all ages. 
	4.Mickey’s Magical Friendship Faire- Disney characters come together in a musical performance.
	5.TRON Lightcycle/Run- a new ride in Disney based on TRON: Legacy.  Fit for all ages for individuals 48 inches and taller. (Fun ride that is like being on a motorcycle).

Epcot

	1. Test Track- While in line you customize a car and when you get to the ride it is a fast coaster where you are in a car, and it goes quick on the track. All ages for individuals 44 inches and taller. 
	2. Remy’s Ratatouille Adventure- New ride in EPCOT.  Any Height and Any age. A 4D experience show. 
	3. Mission: Space- Although for myself I would not try this ride again, due to motion sickness. This ride is a simulation of how it feels to be an astronaut. It's a fun experience but can be intense. They have different “missions” you can take depending on your motion sensitivity. For any age and must be 40 inches or taller. 
	4. Luminous the Symphony of Us- Before the park closing, they have a beautiful water show. 
	5. Guardians of the Galaxy- Cosmic Rewind- New ride in EPCOT.  All ages and for individuals over 48 inches tall. A dark roller coaster ride. One of the largest enclosed roller coasters in the world.

Animal Kingdom

	1.Festival of the Lion King- Play for all ages where they sing, dance, and act your favorite Lion King songs. 
	2.Killimanjaro Safari- Take a ride with your Safari Host to see the animals of Animal Kingdom. 
	3.Avatar Flight of Passage- In my opinion, this ride is one of my favorite rides. You soar the the land of the Avatar in a 3D experience. The ride is for all ages and must be over 44 inches tall. 
	4.It’s Tough to be a Bug- a 3D film where you learn how tough it is to be a bug. Such a wonderful and fun little film for the whole family. 
	5.Wilderness Explorers- For parents with little ones this activity is amazing. It is a scavenger hunt where you go around the park and collect stickers and cards.

Hollywood Studios

	1.Rock n’ Roller Coaster starring Aerosmith- On this fast roller coaster ride you get to ride through the streets of LA listening to Aerosmith. Such a cool ride. Fit for all ages and must be 48 inches tall to ride. 
	2.The Twilight Zone Tower of Terror -
	3.Toy Story Mania- A fun 4D shooting ride and can see all your favorite Toy Story characters. Fit for all ages. 
	4.Millenium Falcon Smugglers Run -   A simulation of flying the Millenium Falcon. A group of riders where each are tasked with a job where you fly the Millenium Falcon. All ages and must be 44 inches tall to ride. 
	5.Beauty and the Beast Live on Stage- Beautiful play where they sing and act Beauty and the Beast.

You may be thinking, “Juliana, is this all of the attractions and activities at the park.”
Of course not, there are many activities and attractions that happen in the park but the ones I have listed are just the top rides and attractions from each park.
I hope this list helped further your Disney planning and one of these rides makes it on your next to do list.

Magic Kingdom

	•	Location of Cinderella’s castle 
	•	Smallest Park in Disney World (but also the most crowded) 
	•	Only two ways to get into Magic Kingdom, Monorail, and by a boat. 
	•	6 Attractions and has the most rides in Disney World. 
	•	Recommend riding the newly added ride, Tron. 
	•	The characters you may see are Stitch, Tinkerbell, Mickey and Minnie Mouse, Donald and Daisy Duck, Goofy, Pooh and Tigger, Ariel, Gaston, Peter Pan, Tiana, Cinderella, and many others. 
	•	Parades throughout the day 
	•	Stage performances throughout the day. 
	•	Do not forget to ride the, “It’s a small world” ride its considered sort of a Disney Rite of Passage. 
	•	In my opinion, the best firework show is presented at this park.

EPCOT

	•	The 3rd largest park 
	•	Walk around the world in EPCOT (you get to experience a view of a few selected countries in this park and includes trying out foods from the country).  
	•	An educational park where you learn about Arts, Science, Technology, and Geography.   
	•	The sphere in the middle of the park named Spaceship Earth.  
	•	Live Entertainment throughout the park.  
	•	Inside Spaceship Earth is a ride 
	•	The firework show includes a beautiful water display in the middle of the park.  
	•	Has rides 5 popular rides my favorite is the test track.  
	•	The characters you find in the park are Finnegan, Pooh, Joy, Sadness, Marvel Characters, and Mickey Mouse.  
	•	My favorite park.

Animal Kingdom 

	•	The largest park in Disney World. 
	•	Home of the Tree of Life 
	•	Animal Kingdom is a zoological theme park. 
	•	Safari rides (is a ride with a guide that will give you a tour around the area where the animals are) 
	•	Visit Pandora: The world of the Avatar where you can ride the Avatar ride which is my favorite ride in the park. 
	•	4 Rides in the park. 
	•	Live entertainment throughout the day.
	•	The 4 stage shows being the popular shows are It’s Tough To Be a Bug and Festival of the Lion King.  
	•	Characters you may see at the park are Donald Duck, Daisy Duck, Simba, Rafiki, Moana, Flik, Timon, Goofy, Pluto, and Mickey Mouse.  
	•	The only park that does not host fireworks for the animals' safety. 
	•	My 2nd favorite park. 

Hollywood Studios

	•	The park is the third largest park in Disney World. 
	•	It has 8 attractions the most popular of the 8 are Star Wars Edge, Toy Story Land, and Sunset Boulevard.
	•	In this park you can learn the history of Walt Disney and the many Disney movies.   
	•	It has a “Hollywood” style movie vibe. 
	•	Has popular rides such as Tower of Terror, Slinky Dog Dash, Millenium Falcon: Smugglers Run, and Aerosmith.  
	•	Parades throughout the day.
	•	Characters that you can find walking through Hollywood Studios are; Woody, Jesse, Storm Troopers, Darth Vader, Chewbacca, Pluto, Donald Duck, Buzz Lightyear, Kylo Ren, Green Army Soldiers, Mickey Mouse, and other special characters may show up through your visit.   
	•	You can catch different stage shows in the park, my favorites being Indiana Jones and Fantasmic!  
	•	Of course, do not miss the Fireworks show that usually happens around 8pm before the park closes.  
	•	My fourth favorite of the 4 parks. 

These are the four parks at Disney World. I do recommend that if you do plan to visit all four parks to spend one day in each. The parks are big and there is just so much to view that you might not get to see the whole park in a day. But regardless of what you decide, all these parks are fun and whether alone or with family and friends it is a wonderful time.
